# 1. Purpose of This Document

This document defines the principal personas for the Ardtire Society Civic Digital Governance Platform.

These personas exist to ensure that product, UX, architecture, permissions, workflows, and operational decisions are grounded in the real kinds of people the platform must serve.

The platform is not being built for an abstract “user.” It is being built for distinct categories of participants with different motivations, permissions, responsibilities, and expectations.

These personas should influence:

- information architecture,
- navigation,
- dashboard design,
- permissions,
- workflow visibility,
- public content strategy,
- governance workflow design,
- moderation tools,
- administrative tools,
- and future product prioritization.

---

# 2. Persona Design Principles

The platform’s personas should be interpreted using the following assumptions:

1. A person may move from one persona state to another over time.
2. Authentication does not equal membership.
3. Membership does not equal operational authority.
4. Operational authority does not necessarily equal constitutional authority.
5. The platform should clearly communicate which persona-state a user is in at any given time.
6. The platform must be legible to both first-time visitors and serious repeat participants.
7. The product should support a progression from low-commitment curiosity to high-commitment civic participation.

---

# 3. Persona Overview

The core personas are:

1. Public Visitor
2. Registered User
3. Membership Applicant
4. Approved Member
5. Governance Contributor / Drafting Member
6. Membership Reviewer
7. Moderator
8. Editor / Publisher
9. Administrator
10. Future Clerk / Governance Officer

These personas overlap in places, but they should still be treated distinctly for product design purposes.

---

# 4. Persona 1: Public Visitor

## Summary

The Public Visitor is someone who has not yet created an account and is primarily trying to understand what the Ardtire Society is, what the platform is for, and whether they may wish to become involved.

This persona is at the very beginning of the participation journey.

## Core questions

The Public Visitor is asking questions such as:

- What is the Ardtire Society?
- What is this platform for?
- Is this a serious institution or just an idea?
- How does participation work?
- Can anyone join?
- What happens if I create an account?
- What is the difference between registering and becoming a member?

## Goals

- Understand the mission and seriousness of the Society.
- Understand the purpose of the platform.
- Understand the participation pathway.
- Read public materials and official explanations.
- Decide whether to register.

## Pain points / risks

- Confusion about what the Society actually is.
- Confusion about whether registration equals membership.
- Concern that the platform may feel vague, improvised, or unserious.
- Difficulty finding authoritative public information.
- Uncertainty about expectations or eligibility.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Clear public explanation.
- Serious and credible presentation.
- Easy-to-find participation pathway.
- Clear call to action.
- Clear explanation of membership as a distinct status.
- Public visibility into enough governance context to establish trust.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I understand what this platform is, what the Society is trying to do, how one becomes involved, and what my next step would be.”

# 5. Persona 2: Registered User

## Summary

The Registered User has created an account and can access authenticated parts of the platform, but is not yet an approved member of the Society.

This persona is no longer merely observing. They have signaled intent to engage, but they do not yet have governance participation rights.

## Core questions

- What can I do now that I have an account?
- Am I already a member?
- What do I need to do next?
- How do I apply for membership?
- What is my current account and participation status?

## Goals

- Access authenticated features.
- Understand current permissions and limitations.
- Begin membership application if desired.
- Track progress toward deeper participation.
- Manage account settings securely.

## Pain points / risks

- Mistaking registration for membership.
- Frustration if authenticated features feel empty or unclear.
- Lack of clarity about next steps.
- Uncertainty about what the platform expects from them.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Clear dashboard or home state after login.
- Explicit status labeling.
- Strong explanation of what registration does and does not provide.
- Simple path into membership application.
- Clear guidance on eligibility, expectations, and process.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I know exactly what my current status is, what I am allowed to do, and what I need to do to become a member.”

# 6. Persona 3: Membership Applicant

## Summary

The Membership Applicant is a registered user who has begun or submitted a membership application and is moving through the review process.

This persona is in a transition state between interest and institutional belonging.

## Core questions

- What information do I need to provide?
- Have I submitted everything?
- What is my application status?
- What happens next?
- Do I need to respond to anything?
- If I am approved, what changes?

## Goals

- Complete the application accurately.
- Submit it successfully.
- Track its status.
- Understand any requests for additional information.
- Know the outcome and next steps.

## Pain points / risks

- Friction in long or unclear forms.
- Fear of losing progress.
- Lack of feedback after submission.
- Anxiety caused by silence or unclear status.
- Unclear reasons for rejection or return.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Save-and-resume workflow.
- Clear required fields.
- Clear submission confirmation.
- Status tracking.
- Notifications for updates.
- Clear explanation of what approval will grant.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“My application process is understandable, I know where it stands, and I know what happens if I am approved or not approved.”

---

# 7. Persona 4: Approved Member

## Summary

The Approved Member has passed the membership process and now has access to member-only platform capabilities.

This persona is part of the Society and is eligible for at least some level of civic participation.

## Core questions

- What member rights do I now have?
- What can I participate in?
- What proposals are active?
- What discussions matter right now?
- What votes are open?
- What is the current constitutional drafting state?

## Goals

- Participate meaningfully in the Society’s civic process.
- Track relevant governance activity.
- Understand current drafting and proposal status.
- Build confidence that the platform reflects a real institutional process.

## Pain points / risks

- Member areas that feel empty, confusing, or unstructured.
- Difficulty identifying what action matters now.
- Unclear difference between proposal draft, active text, and authoritative text.
- Overwhelming governance information without prioritization.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Clear member dashboard.
- Visibility into active proposals, discussions, and voting windows.
- Clarity about their permissions and responsibilities.
- Clear distinction between types of governance content.
- Easy navigation between current work and historical record.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I understand what is happening in the Society’s civic process, where I can contribute, and what the current authoritative state is.”

# 8. Persona 5: Governance Contributor / Drafting Member

## Summary

The Governance Contributor is an approved member who actively participates in constitutional and governance formation by authoring proposals, discussing drafts, revising text, and voting.

This persona represents the core civic worker for the MVP governance loop.

## Core questions

- How do I submit a proposal?
- What stage is my proposal in?
- What comments or changes have been made?
- What is the current draft text?
- When can I vote?
- What happened to a proposal after vote or review?

## Goals

- Draft and submit proposals.
- Participate in structured deliberation.
- Improve text through revision.
- Monitor lifecycle state of proposals.
- Vote on proposals when eligible.
- See how outcomes affect authoritative text.

## Pain points / risks

- Difficulty understanding workflow stages.
- Unclear revision history.
- Difficulty following active discussions.
- Frustration if proposals seem to disappear into unclear process.
- Confusion over what version is being voted on.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Strong proposal creation workflow.
- Clear state labels and transition history.
- Clear revision/version comparison.
- Threaded discussion.
- Voting clarity.
- Explicit linkage between proposal outcomes and authoritative text.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I can propose, discuss, revise, and vote within a clear civic process, and I can understand how decisions affect the institution’s developing constitutional text.”

# 9. Persona 6: Membership Reviewer

## Summary

The Membership Reviewer is a trusted operator responsible for examining membership applications and deciding or advancing them according to policy.

This persona may be a specialized role or a subset of administrative authority.

## Core questions

- Which applications need attention?
- Which are incomplete?
- Which need follow-up?
- How do I record approval, rejection, or return?
- How do I ensure decisions are documented properly?

## Goals

- Process applications efficiently and fairly.
- Maintain consistency in the review process.
- Avoid losing applications in manual backlog.
- Preserve accountability in decisions.

## Pain points / risks

- Application queues with poor filtering or prioritization.
- Lack of consistent workflow states.
- Inability to document reasons clearly.
- Fear of making decisions that are not properly recorded.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Application queue.
- Filters by status, age, and completeness.
- Clean applicant record view.
- Decision tools.
- Request-more-information tools.
- Audit logging for decisions.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I can process applications clearly, consistently, and accountably without relying on external spreadsheets, inboxes, or ad hoc notes.”

# 10. Persona 7: Moderator

## Summary

The Moderator is responsible for maintaining quality, order, and seriousness in discourse and, where applicable, enforcing participation standards and process hygiene.

This persona protects the civic environment from becoming chaotic, abusive, or procedurally disruptive.

## Core questions

- Which content has been flagged?
- Which discussions need intervention?
- What actions can I take?
- How do I enforce standards without damaging legitimacy?
- How is moderation recorded?

## Goals

- Keep discussion constructive and orderly.
- Remove or contain disruptive content or behavior.
- Preserve civic seriousness.
- Avoid arbitrary or opaque enforcement.

## Pain points / risks

- Lack of tools for efficient review.
- Weak auditability of moderation actions.
- Overreach caused by unclear permissions.
- Community distrust if moderation feels invisible or arbitrary.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Flag review tools.
- Content moderation actions.
- User restriction tools where authorized.
- Clear scope of authority.
- Recorded moderation logs.
- Ability to explain or document actions when needed.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I can maintain a constructive civic environment through visible, limited, accountable moderation.”

---

# 11. Persona 8: Editor / Publisher

## Summary

The Editor / Publisher is responsible for preparing and maintaining official public-facing or semi-official content, including explanatory materials, public governance information, and published authoritative text.

This persona helps the institution speak clearly and publish responsibly.

## Core questions

- What content is draft versus official?
- What is ready for publication?
- What is currently authoritative?
- How do I update public-facing materials without causing confusion?

## Goals

- Publish clear and credible public content.
- Distinguish draft materials from official materials.
- Keep public information current and coherent.
- Reflect governance developments accurately.

## Pain points / risks

- Confusion between proposal content and official content.
- Weak publishing workflow.
- Public-facing inconsistencies.
- Lack of traceability for published changes.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Controlled publication states.
- Clear distinction between internal workflow objects and public publication objects.
- Strong labeling of authoritative content.
- Editing and review tools.
- Publication history.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I can keep the public face of the institution coherent, accurate, and trustworthy.”

---

# 12. Persona 9: Administrator

## Summary

The Administrator is responsible for platform operations, permissions, exceptional cases, and institutional system health at the product and workflow level.

This persona needs broad visibility and carefully bounded power.

## Core questions

- What requires operational attention right now?
- What users, applications, proposals, or records need intervention?
- Are permissions and statuses correct?
- What changed recently?
- Are there workflow anomalies or edge cases that need resolution?

## Goals

- Keep the platform operationally healthy.
- Ensure statuses and permissions reflect institutional reality.
- Resolve edge cases cleanly.
- Maintain auditability and trust.
- Reduce manual administrative burden.

## Pain points / risks

- Operational sprawl across disconnected tools.
- Poor visibility into pending work.
- Unclear exceptional-case handling.
- Risk of overpowered admin actions without adequate audit.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Admin dashboard.
- Search and filtering across major objects.
- Safe status/role management tools.
- Exceptional-case intervention tools.
- Audit visibility.
- Platform-level operational awareness.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I can manage the platform, resolve exceptional cases, and maintain operational clarity without undermining procedural legitimacy.”

# 13. Persona 10: Future Clerk / Governance Officer

## Summary

The Future Clerk / Governance Officer represents a later-phase institutional role responsible for preserving process correctness, records, outcomes, and formal governance continuity.

This persona may not be essential to the earliest MVP, but the product should be designed with this future role in mind.

## Core questions

- What is the formal status of this item?
- What rules governed this outcome?
- What version was under consideration?
- What official record should be preserved?
- How do I reconstruct the institutional history of a provision or proposal?

## Goals

- Preserve formal process integrity.
- Preserve the institutional record.
- Support certification and publication of outcomes.
- Make procedural history reconstructable.

## Pain points / risks

- Incomplete records.
- Missing links between workflow objects and official outputs.
- Outcomes without explicit rule references.
- Historical ambiguity.

## What this persona needs from the platform

- Durable records.
- Strong auditability.
- State transition history.
- Outcome records.
- Version history.
- Publication record integrity.

## Success for this persona

This persona succeeds when they can say:

“I can reconstruct, certify, and preserve the institutional history of governance actions with confidence.”

# 14. Cross-Persona Insights

Several important product insights arise across all personas.

## 14.1 Status clarity is essential
Nearly every persona needs to understand status:
- account status,
- membership status,
- proposal status,
- publication status,
- voting status,
- and authority status.

## 14.2 The platform must distinguish roles clearly
The product must never blur:
- visitor,
- registered user,
- applicant,
- member,
- moderator,
- reviewer,
- editor,
- and administrator.

## 14.3 Trust comes from legibility
Users trust the platform when they can understand:
- what it is,
- where they are,
- what they can do,
- what happens next,
- and what records exist.

## 14.4 The member contributor is the heart of the governance product
The platform’s deepest value is proven when approved members can contribute to constitutional drafting in a serious, structured, intelligible process.

## 14.5 Operators need first-class tooling
The platform will fail operationally if reviewer, moderator, editor, and administrator workflows are treated as afterthoughts.

# 15. Persona Progression Model

The intended progression for many users is:

1. Public Visitor
2. Registered User
3. Membership Applicant
4. Approved Member
5. Governance Contributor

A smaller subset may later also become:

- Membership Reviewer,
- Moderator,
- Editor / Publisher,
- Administrator,
- or Future Clerk / Governance Officer.

The platform should make this progression feel coherent and intentional.

---

# 16. Primary Persona Priorities for MVP

The MVP should prioritize excellent support for the following personas first:

1. Public Visitor
2. Registered User
3. Membership Applicant
4. Approved Member
5. Governance Contributor
6. Membership Reviewer
7. Administrator

Moderation and editorial workflows should also exist, but the first product proof comes from making the civic progression and governance loop function reliably.

# 17. Persona Summary

This platform is fundamentally serving three layers of people:

## Public layer
People trying to understand and possibly enter the institution.

## Participation layer
People moving through identity, membership, and governance contribution.

## Operator layer
People responsible for keeping the institution’s digital process coherent, safe, accurate, and durable.

The platform succeeds when each of those layers can act confidently inside a system that clearly reflects who they are, what they may do, and how the civic process works.
